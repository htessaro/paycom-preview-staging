A fraud warning (also known as a TC40 report for Visa or a SAFE report for Mastercard) is a notification from a card scheme that a transaction has been reported as fraudulent by the cardholder or their issuing bank.

## What is a fraud warning?

Fraud warnings differ from formal disputes in one critical way: they are **non-financial** messages.

<Callout type="info">
When you receive a fraud warning, no funds are withdrawn from your account. The warning is simply an information message from the card network indicating that the cardholder has flagged the transaction as unauthorized.
</Callout>

## Why they matter

While fraud warnings don't cost you money immediately, they are highly accurate predictors of future formal disputes. Approximately **80% or more** of transactions flagged in TC40 or SAFE reports eventually turn into formal chargebacks.

Monitoring these warnings allows you to:

*   **Identitfy High-Risk Patterns:** Spot fraud rings or specific product vulnerabilities early.
*   **Avoid Dispute Fees:** By refunding the transaction before it becomes a chargeback, you save on the administrative fees charged for a formal bank challenge.
*   **Protect Your Merchant Account:** High chargeback rates can lead to fines or account suspension. Proactive refunds keep your ratios healthy.

## Merchant actions

When a fraud warning appears in your **Fraud Warnings** dashboard or arrives via a webhook:

1.  **Review the Transaction:** Check the customer's history and shipping status. If the product hasn't shipped yet, cancel the order immediately.
2.  **Issue a Proactive Refund:** If the transaction looks suspicious, the safest action is to refund the transaction amount.
3.  **Update Blocklists:** Add the customer's email or device fingerprint to your [Risk Rules](/docs/risk-disputes-fraud/risk-rules) to prevent future attempts.

## API integration

You can automate your fraud prevention logic by listening for the `fraud_warning.created` webhook. Learn more about how to set up and manage [Webhooks](/docs/payments/webhooks).

```json
{
  "id": "fw_456...",
  "type": "fraud_warning.created",
  "data": {
    "object": "fraud_warning",
    "charge": "ch_123...",
    "fraud_type": "stolen_card",
    "reported_at": "2024-03-24T14:45:00Z"
  }
}
```

<Callout type="warn">
Once you refund a transaction flagged by a fraud warning, the cardholder's bank can no longer file a formal chargeback for that transaction. This is the most effective way to protect your account.
</Callout>
