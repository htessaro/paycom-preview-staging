Pay.com offers four integration paths. The right one depends on how much control you want over the customer-facing checkout experience and what PCI compliance your business already has in place.

All four paths share the same underlying platform and token vault, so you can start with one approach and move to another later without re-integrating.

## Quick comparison

| Integration | Who builds the checkout UI | PCI scope | Best for |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Universal SDK** | Pay.com | Minimal | Fastest time to market; no design customization needed |
| **Headless SDK** | You | Minimal | Full brand control without taking on full PCI compliance |
| **Server-to-Server API** | You | Full (PCI required) | Maximum backend control; large organizations with existing compliance |
| **Tokenization Only** | You (card capture only) | Minimal | Storing cards for recurring charges or anonymous checkouts |

## Universal SDK

Pay.com renders the entire payment UI. Your team adds the SDK to your page and Pay.com handles everything else: the form, the styling, the validation, and the submission.

This is the fastest path to accepting payments. Your PCI scope is minimal because no sensitive card data touches your systems.

Choose this path if you want to go live quickly and don't need a fully custom checkout design.

## Headless SDK

You build the payment UI using your own components. The Headless SDK handles all data transmission and security in the background, so sensitive card data never passes through your servers.

Your PCI scope stays minimal, while you keep full control over the customer experience.

<Callout type="info">
The Headless SDK is one of Pay.com's key differentiators. Most payment platforms require you to choose between their pre-built UI (easy, but constrained) or building your own (flexible, but full PCI burden). The Headless SDK removes that trade-off.
</Callout>

Choose this path if your brand experience matters and you have a design team ready to build a custom checkout.

## Server-to-Server API

Your servers handle card data directly and communicate with Pay.com's API. You have maximum control over every aspect of the payment flow and checkout experience.

This path requires full PCI compliance because card details pass through your infrastructure.

Choose this path if you're PCI-certified, have complex backend payment logic, or are building for an enterprise environment with specific security requirements.

## Tokenization Only SDK

The SDK captures and tokenizes card details on the client side. Your backend then transacts using those tokens without ever handling raw card data.

This is useful for public or anonymous checkouts where you want to store a payment method for use later, without requiring the customer to be logged in.

Choose this path if you need to save payment methods for future charges, subscriptions, or deferred billing flows.

## Not sure which to choose?

If you're starting fresh and don't have strong requirements in either direction, start with the Universal SDK. You can always move to the Headless SDK or API path later as your product matures, without rebuilding your Pay.com integration.

Your Pay.com implementation team can advise on the right path for your setup during onboarding. See [get started](/documentation/introduction/get-started) for how to reach them.

Once you've chosen a path, the [payment guides](/documentation/payments/guides/create-a-charge) and [SDK documentation](/documentation/payments/sdk/pay-com-sdks-overview) walk through the technical implementation.
