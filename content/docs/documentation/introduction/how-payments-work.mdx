When a customer pays through your checkout, Pay.com acts as the central layer between your business, your acquiring banks, and the card networks. Its job is to find the fastest path to an approved transaction, handle authentication where required, and report the final result back to you.

## Smart routing and orchestration

Pay.com connects your business to multiple acquiring banks simultaneously. When a payment comes in, the platform's routing logic selects the acquirer most likely to approve it at the best rate, based on factors like card type, geography, transaction amount, and historical approval patterns.

```
Your checkout
      │
      ▼
  Pay.com
      │
      ├──► Acquirer A ──► Card network ──► Customer's bank
      │                                         │
      ├──► Acquirer B (failover)                │
      │                                    Approved / Declined
      └──► Acquirer C (failover)                │
                                                ▼
                                          Pay.com returns result
                                          to your checkout
```

If the selected acquirer is unavailable or declines the transaction, Pay.com retries automatically with the next best option. This failover happens fast enough that the customer doesn't notice. From your business's perspective, you get a single result: approved or declined.

This is what makes multi-acquirer orchestration different from a traditional single-acquirer gateway. A gateway that routes through only one acquirer fails entirely when that acquirer has an outage or an unusually high decline rate. Pay.com routes around those failures automatically.

Most merchants rely on Pay.com to configure their routing setup. More advanced merchants use the Pay.com dashboard to define their own routing rules and traffic splits. For a deeper look at orchestration configuration, see the [orchestration](/documentation/orchestration) section.

## Payment methods

Pay.com supports three broad categories of payment methods. You select which to offer from the Pay.com dashboard, and the platform manages the underlying provider relationships. You can add or remove methods at any time without changing your integration code.

- **Cards** — Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and other major networks, globally. Includes 3DS2 authentication and automatic card updating when cards expire.
- **Digital wallets** — Apple Pay and Google Pay, completing without a redirect. Best for mobile-heavy checkout traffic.
- **Alternative payment methods** — a growing set of regional and local methods, including prepaid vouchers, bank redirects, and Buy Now Pay Later options.

Pay.com also supports outbound payments (payouts) to cards and select wallets. Payout availability varies by method and market.

For a full list of supported methods with capability details and setup guides, see the [payment methods](/documentation/payment-methods) section.

## Transaction lifecycle

Every payment in Pay.com moves through a defined set of states from creation to settlement. Key points in the lifecycle:

- **Authorization** — the acquirer checks with the card network and issuing bank that funds are available
- **Capture** — funds are moved from the customer's account (immediate by default, or deferred if you use a hold-and-capture flow)
- **Settlement** — cleared funds are transferred to your account on your settlement schedule

For a complete walkthrough of transaction states and how holds, refunds, and disputes affect them, see [payment lifecycle overview](/documentation/paycom-concepts/payment-lifecycle-overview).
