A refund returns settled funds from a previously successful charge back to the customer. Unlike canceling a [hold](/docs/api-reference/holds/hold-cancel), which releases reserved funds almost instantly, a refund moves actual money and may take several business days to appear on the customer's statement.

## Requirements

* The `id` of the successful charge you want to refund (`chrg_...`).
* Access to your [API credentials](/docs/api-reference/authentication).

<Steps>
<Step>
## Send the refund request

Call the [`POST /v1/refunds`](/docs/api-reference/refunds/create-refund) endpoint with the charge ID and the amount to return. Amounts are in the smallest currency unit, so `10000` equals $100.00 USD.

Pass the charge ID in the `charge` field and the amount in `amount_to_refund`. You can omit `amount_to_refund` to refund the full remaining balance.

<Step>
## Check the refund status

Check the `status` field in the response:

* `succeeded`: The refund was accepted and the funds are being returned to the customer.
* `failed`: The refund was declined. This can happen if the original payment method is no longer valid or the refund window has closed. The `failure_code` and `failure_message` fields explain the reason.

</Step>
<Step>
## Listen for webhooks

Subscribe to these events to track the outcome of each refund:

* `refund.succeeded`: The funds are being returned to the customer.
* `refund.failed`: The refund was declined. Use this to alert your support team for manual review.
* `charge.refunded`: Fires on every successful refund, full or partial. The `refunded` boolean is `true` when the charge balance reaches zero.

<Callout type="warn">
Always verify webhook signatures using the `Pay-Signature` header and your endpoint's secret key (via HMAC SHA-256) to ensure the event is legitimate.
</Callout>

</Step>
