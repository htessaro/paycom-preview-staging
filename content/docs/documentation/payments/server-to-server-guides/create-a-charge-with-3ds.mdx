The 3D Secure (3DS) flow allows merchants to authenticate credit and debit card transactions through the customer's bank to reduce fraud and satisfy regulatory requirements.
While the SDK handles this process automatically, the API-only flow requires the merchant to manage the authentication parameters and customer redirection.

## Requirements
To successfully initiate a 3DS charge, ensure your [environment](/docs/api-reference/environments) is configured and you have collected the necessary data points.

Before making your first call, complete these technical prerequisites:

* [Select your environment Base URL](/docs/api-reference/environments).
* [Have access to your API credentials](/docs/api-reference/authentication).
* [Generate a unique `idempotency-key` header for each request](/docs/api-reference/idempotency-key).

## Create a charge with 3DS

To create a charge with 3DS, you need to send a `POST` request to the [`/v1/charges`](/docs/api-reference/charges/create-charge) endpoint including the following data:

* **Browser metadata:** Standard industry parameters required under `source_data.card.authentication_context` (all fields are mandatory).
* **Customer identity:** The customer's email address is required under `source_data.billing_details`. Full billing details are highly recommended to increase approval chances.
* **Transaction details:** The currency, amount, and payment source (raw card details or a stored token).
* **Challenge enforcement:** To explicitly enforce a 3DS challenge, you must send the `payment_method_options.card.request_threed_secure` parameter with the value set to `challenge`.

<Callout type="info">
If this charge is the initial transaction for a recurring payment sequence, add `"setup_future_usage": "off_session"` inside your `source_data` object.
</Callout>

Depending on the bank's response, the API will return one of two possible outcomes:

* **`requires_authentication`:** The transaction requires a bank challenge to satisfy SCA (Strong Customer Authentication) requirements. The cardholder must be verified before the charge can proceed. Continue to Step 2.
* **`succeeded`:** The bank authenticated the user behind the scenes (frictionless flow) and the funds were captured. No further steps are required.

If you receive an API error response, the card was declined or the provided data was invalid.

<Callout type="info">
**External 3DS (Advanced)**
If you use an external 3DS provider or your own MPI, you can pass the pre-authenticated data directly to Pay.com. In this flow, you must provide the `ds_trans_id` and other 3DS objects received from your software provider in the initial charge request. Pay.com will pass this data to the issuer for validation without triggering a new challenge.
</Callout>

## Create a linked authentication session

When the charge status is `requires_authentication`, you must link it to a new authentication session.

Submit a `POST` request to [`/v1/sessions/authentication/linked`](/docs/api-reference/authenticationsessions/create-linked-authentication-session) with the following parameters:

* `resource`: The charge ID returned in Step 1.
* `return_url`: The URL to redirect the customer to after the challenge completes.
* `confirm`: Controls when Pay.com attempts the final authorization:
  * `true` (recommended): Pay.com authorizes the charge immediately after the customer completes the bank challenge, then sends a `charge.succeeded` or `charge.failed` webhook. Use this if your fulfillment logic is webhook-driven.
  * `false`: Pay.com redirects the customer to your `return_url` without capturing funds. Your server must then call the [`/confirm`](/docs/api-reference/charges/confirm-charge) endpoint to finalize. Use this when you need to run internal checks (such as fraud scoring or inventory verification) between 3DS completion and authorization.

<Step>
## Redirect the customer

Once the linked authentication session is created, redirect the customer to the `url` field returned in the Step 2 response to present the 3DS challenge.

The customer experience will vary by device:
* **Browser-based:** The challenge opens within the current window or a redirect.
* **Mobile-based:** The SDK browser handles a full-flow adjustment for the mobile screen.

</Step>
<Step>
## Finalize the transaction

After the customer completes the bank challenge, the exact order of operations depends on your `confirm` setting from Step 2:

* **If you used `confirm: true`:** Pay.com authorizes the charge before redirecting the customer. Listen for webhooks to fulfill the order. Do not rely solely on the customer reaching your `return_url`, as a browser crash could prevent the redirect while the charge still succeeds.
* **If you used `confirm: false`:** Pay.com redirects the customer to your `return_url` without capturing funds. Once the customer lands on your `return_url`, finalize the charge by sending a `POST` request to [`/v1/charges/{charge_id}/confirm`](/docs/api-reference/charges/confirm-charge).

## Listen for webhooks

Because authorization happens asynchronously after the bank challenge, use webhooks to trigger your fulfillment logic regardless of which `confirm` mode you used.

Subscribe to the following events:

* `charge.succeeded`: The funds were successfully captured. Use this to fulfill the order.
* `charge.failed`: The charge was declined after the challenge, for example due to insufficient funds or a failed 3DS result.

<Callout type="warn">
Always verify webhook signatures using the `Pay-Signature` header and your endpoint's secret key (via HMAC SHA-256) to ensure the event is legitimate.
</Callout>

</Step>
