Collaborations (also known as pre-dispute alerts) are early warning services provided by
card networks and third-party vendors. When a cardholder disputes a transaction at a
participating bank, an alert is sent to Pay.com before a formal chargeback is filed.
Pay.com delivers a `collaboration.open` webhook event to your endpoint and updates the
**Collaborations** tab in the dashboard, giving you a short window to refund the
transaction and close the case without it counting against your dispute ratios. You can
respond to the alert through the dashboard or the
[Collaborations API](/api-reference/collaborations).

## How collaborations work

When a participating bank receives a cardholder challenge, it sends an alert to Pay.com 
instead of immediately opening a dispute. The response window varies, but you typically 
have 24–72 hours to act. Your deadline is shown in the **Collaborations** tab under the 
**Refundable Until** column.

<Steps>
<Step>
### Alert received
Pay.com receives the alert from the card network or vendor and notifies you via the
dashboard or a `collaboration.open` webhook event.
</Step>
<Step>
### Resolution decision
You decide whether to refund the transaction or let it escalate to a formal dispute.
</Step>
<Step>
### Outcome
If you refund, the case is closed and no chargeback is recorded against your account. 
If you escalate or do nothing, the dispute proceeds through the standard chargeback 
process.
</Step>
</Steps>

Alerts that are resolved with a refund do not count toward your dispute-to-transaction 
ratio in card network monitoring programs. This makes collaborations one of the most 
effective tools for merchants who are approaching or managing threshold limits.

## Supported services

Pay.com integrates with the leading collaboration services across major card networks:

| Service | Networks covered | How it works |
|---|---|---|
| **Visa (RDR)** | Visa | Visa's automated pre-dispute service. You define refund rules and Pay.com resolves matching alerts automatically, without manual review. |
| **Mastercard Collaboration** | Mastercard | Mastercard's native pre-chargeback resolution service for manual or automated responses. |
| **Ethoca** | Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Discover, and others | A third-party service owned by Mastercard that provides alerts across multiple networks, giving you the broadest possible coverage. |

## Resolution modes

You can handle collaboration alerts in two ways depending on your setup:

| Mode | How it works | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Automated** | You define rules, for example always refund if the amount is below $50, and Pay.com resolves matching alerts automatically. Available for Visa transactions via RDR. | High alert volumes where manual review isn't scalable. |
| **Manual review** | You review individual alerts in the Collaborations section of the dashboard and decide case by case. | Lower volumes or situations requiring human judgment on each transaction. |

To enroll in collaboration services or configure your automated refund rules, contact 
Pay.com Support. Our risk team will help you set up the right configuration for your 
business.

<Callout type="warn">
Collaboration alerts have strict response windows, typically 24–72 hours. If you do
not act before the window closes, the alert escalates to a formal dispute automatically.
</Callout>

## Webhook events

Pay.com sends the following events as a collaboration alert moves through its lifecycle:

| Event | When it fires |
|---|---|
| `collaboration.open` | A new collaboration alert has been received. |
| `collaboration.resolved` | The alert was resolved with a refund. |
| `collaboration.escalated` | The alert was not resolved in time and escalated to a formal dispute. |

For instructions on configuring endpoints and verifying payloads, see
[Webhook events](/docs/payments/webhooks/webhook-events).
