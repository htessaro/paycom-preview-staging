A dispute (also known as a chargeback) occurs when a cardholder contacts their issuing bank
to challenge a transaction. The bank initiates the dispute through the card network, which
notifies Pay.com. Pay.com then delivers a `dispute.open` webhook event to your endpoint and
updates the **Disputes** page in the dashboard. From that point, you have a fixed window of
time to accept or defend the dispute, either through the dashboard or the
[Disputes API](/api-reference/disputes).

## Dispute lifecycle

When a dispute is initiated, it moves through a series of stages defined by the card 
network (Visa, Mastercard, and so on).

<Steps>
<Step>
### Dispute initiated
The cardholder challenges the transaction with their bank. The bank opens the dispute
through the card network, and Pay.com notifies you by delivering a `dispute.open` webhook
event to your endpoint and updating the **Disputes** page in the dashboard. You can also
retrieve disputes programmatically via the
[List disputes](/api-reference/disputes/list-disputes) endpoint.

Depending on the card network and dispute type, funds are either temporarily held or
immediately reversed at this point. This determines when you lose access to the disputed
amount.
</Step>
<Step>
### Evidence required
You have a fixed window to decide how to respond, as defined by the card network. This 
deadline is shown as **Expires in** on the **Disputes** page. If you do not respond 
before this deadline, the dispute is automatically resolved in favor of the cardholder.
</Step>
<Step>
### Representment
If you choose to defend, you submit evidence through the dashboard or via the 
[Update dispute evidence](/api-reference/disputes/update-dispute-evidence) endpoint. 
Pay.com forwards your evidence to the issuing bank to re-present the original charge.

<Callout type="info">
You can submit evidence for multiple disputes in a single request using the 
[Bulk defend disputes](/api-reference/disputes/bulk-defend-disputes) endpoint.
</Callout>
</Step>
<Step>
### Decision
The issuing bank reviews the evidence and issues a final ruling. If the ruling is in 
your favor, the held funds are returned to you. If not, the dispute closes in favor of 
the cardholder.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Accepting or defending

When you receive a dispute, you have two options:

| Action | What it means | When to use it |
|---|---|---|
| **Accept** | You concede the dispute and the funds are returned to the cardholder. | The transaction was erroneous, the customer is entitled to a refund, or you lack sufficient evidence to defend. |
| **Defend** | You submit evidence to challenge the dispute through the card network. | You have proof of delivery, service usage, customer communication, or agreement to your terms. |

Accepting a dispute is final and cannot be undone. It is functionally similar to a refund 
but typically carries additional fees from the card network.

## Evidence for disputes

To successfully defend a dispute, submit as much relevant documentation as possible. The 
table below shows the most common evidence types accepted by card networks:

| Evidence type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Proof of delivery** | Tracking numbers, carrier confirmations, signed delivery receipts |
| **Proof of service** | Service logs, login history, digital download timestamps |
| **Customer communication** | Emails or chat logs referencing the purchase or delivery |
| **Terms acceptance** | Signed agreements or checkout confirmation showing the customer accepted your refund policy |

## Webhook events

Pay.com sends events as a dispute moves through its lifecycle. The key events to
subscribe to are:

| Event | When it fires |
|---|---|
| `dispute.open` | A new dispute has been opened. |
| `dispute.expired` | The response deadline passed without action and the dispute was automatically resolved in favor of the cardholder. |
| `dispute.chargeback_accepted` | The merchant accepted the dispute. |
| `dispute.won` | The issuing bank accepted the defense and the funds were returned. |
| `dispute.lost` | The issuing bank rejected the defense. |

For the full list of dispute events, see [Webhook events](/docs/payments/webhooks/webhook-events).

The dispute object includes an `expires_at` timestamp showing the date by which you must
respond. If you miss this deadline, the dispute is automatically resolved in favor of the
cardholder, even if you have valid evidence to submit.

## Next steps

The best dispute is one that never gets filed. Explore how to get ahead of 
them earlier in the process:

- [Collaborations](/docs/risk-disputes-fraud/collaborations): use early resolution 
  services like Ethoca and RDR to resolve transactions before they become formal disputes.
- [Fraud warnings](/docs/risk-disputes-fraud/fraud-warnings): monitor fraud signals early 
  to act before a dispute is filed.
- [Risk rules](/docs/risk-disputes-fraud/risk-rules): configure custom rules to block 
  suspicious transactions automatically.
