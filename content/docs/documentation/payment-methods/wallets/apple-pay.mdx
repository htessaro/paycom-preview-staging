Apple Pay is a digital wallet payment method that lets customers pay at checkout using a
card saved to their Apple Wallet. It is widely used across the Americas, Europe,
Asia-Pacific, the Middle East, and parts of Africa.

Enabling Apple Pay removes card entry from checkout. Customers authenticate with Face ID,
Touch ID, or their device passcode and pay in one step.

<Callout type="info">
  Apple Pay is available to cardholders whose banks support it in eligible countries. For more details, see Apple’s list of [participating banks](https://support.apple.com/en-us/109524).
</Callout>

## Payment method properties

This table outlines the key properties and limitations of using Apple Pay, including where it is supported, how it is processed, and transaction constraints.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Payment type** | In-page (no full-page redirect; a native sheet overlays the merchant's checkout page) |
| **Merchant countries** | Australia, Austria, Belgium, Brazil, Bulgaria,Canada,Croatia,Cyprus,Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Faroe Islands, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Guernsey, Hong Kong, Hungary, Iceland,I Ireland, Italy, Japan, Jersey, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Monaco, Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Singapore, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, UK, US, United Arab Emirates, Vatican City |
| **Customer countries** | Global. Customer must have an Apple device with a card added to Apple Wallet. |
| **Currencies** | AUD, BRL, CAD, CHF, CNY, CZK, DKK, EUR, GBP, HKD, HUF, INR, JPY, MXN, MYR, NOK, NZD, PHP, PLN, RON, SEK, SGD, THB, TWD, USD |
| **Transaction minimum** | $0.01 |
| **Transaction maximum** | No limit |
| **Payment confirmation** | Immediate. The charge is authorized and captured at the moment the customer authenticates. |
| **Settlement timing** | Next business day |
| **Refunds** | Fully and partially supported|
| **Disputes** | Fully supported. See [Disputes](#disputes) |
| **Recurring payments** | Fully supported. See [Recurring payments](#recurring-payments) |
| **Vaulting / saved credentials** | Not supported. Apple Pay generates a device-specific token per transaction |
| **3DS / SCA** | Handled automatically. Cryptogram 3DS authentication is performed by the device at the time of payment. |
| **Redirect required** | No. The Apple Pay sheet appears as a native overlay on the customer's device. |


## Customer payment flow

Here's how Apple Pay authorizes a payment from your checkout page:

<Steps>
<Step>
### Checkout page rendering
When the customer reaches the checkout page, Pay.com detects Apple Pay
availability in their browser and renders the Apple Pay button.
</Step>

<Step>
### Payment initiation
The customer taps or clicks the Apple Pay button.
</Step>

<Step>
### Sheet presentation
An Apple Pay sheet appears, sliding up from the bottom on mobile, or as an
overlay on desktop Safari. It displays the merchant name, order amount, and
the customer's default Apple Wallet card.
</Step>

<Step>
### Card selection
The customer confirms their card or selects a different one from their wallet.
</Step>

<Step>
### Biometric authentication
The customer authenticates the payment using their Face ID, or their device passcode.
</Step>

<Step>
### Payment confirmation
The Apple Pay sheet closes and the customer sees a payment confirmation on the page.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
If authentication fails or the customer cancels, the Apple Pay sheet dismisses without creating a charge. The customer remains on your checkout page.
</Callout>

## Apple Pay setup and integration

### Before you begin

Make sure the following requirements are configured:

- You must have an active Pay.com merchant account with Apple Pay enabled. Contact your account manager or enable Apple Pay in the Payment methods section of your Pay.com dashboard.
- Register and verify your domain. See [Apple's documentation on configuring your environment](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/apple_pay_on_the_web/configuring_your_environment) for instructions.
- Apple Pay on the web requires **Safari on macOS (Sierra or later)** or **Safari on iOS (10 or later)**. It is not supported in Chrome, Firefox, or other non-Safari browsers.

<Callout type="note">
You do not need a separate Apple Merchant ID or Apple Developer account. Pay.com handles Apple merchant registration, certificate management, and validation on your behalf.
</Callout>


### Dashboard configuration

Apple Pay is configured in your Pay.com dashboard. In some cases it may already be enabled by default, if not follow the steps below to turn it on.

<Steps>
<Step>
#### Open account settings
Click your profile name in the top-right corner of your
[Pay.com dashboard](https://dashboard.pay.com) and select Settings.
</Step>

<Step>
#### Enable Apple Pay
Navigate to Payment methods and turn on Apple Pay.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Integration paths

Pay.com supports three integration paths for Apple Pay. Choose based on how
much control you need over the checkout experience.


<Tabs items={['SDK', 'API']}>
<Tab value="SDK">

### Universal SDK

Apple Pay renders automatically within the universal web checkout. No additional
code is required beyond the standard SDK initialization. See the
[Get started with the Pay.com SDK](/docs/payments/sdks/integrate-the-pay-com-sdk)
guide for setup instructions. Use the universal SDK when you want Pay.com to handle the entire payment flow including the UI.

### Headless SDK

Use the Headless SDK when you want to control the user interface and let the payment logic be 
handled automatically by Pay.com. You render your own Apple Pay button and
control when the payment sheet appears.

Apple Pay is not available in all browsers. Call `canMakePayments()` after initializing the
checkout instance and only show the button if the result includes
`applepay: true`.

```javascript
const canMakePaymentsResult = await checkout.canMakePayments()
// Example result: { applepay: true, googlepay: false }

if (canMakePaymentsResult.applepay) {
  // Show your Apple Pay button
}
```

**Trigger the payment when the customer taps the button.**

```javascript
async function handleApplePay() {
  await checkout.headless('applepay')
}
```

<Callout type="info">
Apple's Human Interface Guidelines require that you use the official Apple Pay
button style. Use the CSS property `-apple-pay-button` or Apple's
[button guidelines](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/apple-pay)
to render the button. Do not substitute a generic button styled to look like
Apple Pay.
</Callout>

</Tab>
<Tab value="API">

Use the native Apple Pay SDK on your frontend to prompt the customer for payment. Once the customer authorizes, the wallet provider returns a payment object to your client-side code. Pass the token data from that object to your backend, then make your request to the `POST /v1/charges` endpoint.
Pay.com provides two ways to send the wallet data to your backend:

### Pay.com decrypts the payload

Pass the raw encrypted blob returned by the [Apple Pay SDK](https://developer.apple.com/apple-pay/implementation/) directly to
Pay.com. 

Send a `POST /v1/charges` request with `source_data.type` set to `"applepay"` and pass the full encrypted object from the wallet provider. Pay.com handles decryption of the token and processing of the payment.

<Callout type="info">
This flow requires a real user payment session on an Apple device. It cannot be tested server-side in isolation, you must trigger an Apple Pay payment from Safari on a real device (or a registered test device in sandbox).
</Callout>

### You decrypt the payload

Send a `POST /v1/charges` request with `source_data.type` set to `"network_token"` and provide the decrypted token details inside `source_data.network_token`. Amounts are in the smallest currency unit, so `10000` equals $100.00 USD.
Always pass billing details from the Apple Pay response into
`source_data.billing_details`. This improves authorization rates by giving
the issuer address verification data.

The `token`, `expiry_month`, `expiry_year`, `eci`, and `cryptogram` values come from the decrypted `paymentData` object returned by the Apple Pay authorization response on the device. Map the billing address from the Apple Pay `onpaymentauthorized` event to
Pay.com fields as follows:

| Apple Pay field | Pay.com parameters |
|:---|:---|
| `payment.billingContact.addressLines` | `address.line1` |
| `payment.billingContact.locality` | `address.city` |
| `payment.billingContact.postalCode` | `address.postal_code` |
| `payment.billingContact.administrativeArea` | `address.state` |
| `payment.billingContact.countryCode` | `address.country` |

## Testing

To test Apple Pay through Pay.com checkout, use a real Apple device with a card added to Apple Wallet and your Pay.com test API keys. This lets you simulate successful payments without charging the card.

| Scenario | Test action | Expected result |
|---|---|---|
| Successful payment | Authenticate with Face ID or Apple passcode | `status: succeeded`, `wallet.type: "applepay"` in response |
| Customer cancels | Tap Cancel on the Apple Pay sheet | Sheet dismisses, no charge created. |
| Unavailable in browser | Open checkout in Chrome or Firefox | Apple Pay button does not render |

## Handling payment events

Subscribe to the following webhook events to track Apple Pay charge outcomes. 

| Webhook event | Trigger condition | Recommended action |
|---|---|---|
| `charge.succeeded` | The charge was authorized and captured successfully | Fulfill the order, send a confirmation email |
| `charge.failed` | The charge was declined by the issuer or by Apple Pay | Notify the customer and prompt them to use a different card or payment method. |

Because Apple Pay confirmation is immediate (synchronous), you will not 
encounter a `charge.pending` state for standard Apple Pay transactions. The 
`charge.succeeded` event is the only event you need to fulfill an order.

<Callout type="warn">
Always verify webhook signatures using the `Pay-Signature` header and your 
endpoint's secret key (HMAC SHA-256) before acting on any event. See the 
[Webhooks overview](/payments/webhooks) for verification instructions.
</Callout>

## Refunds

Apple Pay charges can be refunded in full or partially. Refunds are processed
against the underlying card, not the Apple Wallet. The customer sees the
refund credited to the card linked to their Apple Wallet.

See the [refund a charge](/payments/server-to-server-guides/refund-a-charge)
guide for the full request format, partial refund instructions, and webhook events.

## Disputes

Apple Pay transactions are backed by the customer's underlying card and follow
that card network's dispute and chargeback rules. When a cardholder contacts
their bank to challenge a transaction, Pay.com notifies you immediately and
updates your dashboard.

### Dispute notifications

When a dispute is opened, Pay.com:

- Delivers a `dispute.open` webhook event to your endpoint
- Updates the Disputes page in your dashboard
- Shows a response deadline in the `expires_at` field

You must respond before the `expires_at` deadline. If you do not, the dispute
is automatically resolved in favor of the cardholder.

### Responding to disputes

You have two options when a dispute is opened:

| Action | When to use it |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Accept** | The transaction was erroneous, the customer is entitled to a refund, or you lack sufficient evidence to defend. |
| **Defend** | You have proof of delivery, service usage, customer communication, or the customer's agreement to your terms. |

Because Apple Pay uses a device-specific token (DPAN) rather than the
customer's real card number, dispute records in Pay.com and on your card
network statement will reference the underlying card, not the Apple Pay
wallet.

For the full dispute lifecycle, evidence requirements, and webhook events, see
the [disputes guide](/payments/disputes).

## Recurring payments

When Pay.com processes an Apple Pay
transaction, it decrypts the device-specific token (DPAN) and stores the
underlying card used for the payment as a standard payment method (`pm_card_...`). This stored
card behaves identically to any other saved card on Pay.com and can be used
for both merchant-initiated charges and subscription billing.

<Callout type="info">
  The `pm_card_...` ID is available in the charge response regardless of
  which Apple Pay integration path you used — Universal SDK, Headless SDK,
  or directly via the API using either the encrypted Apple Pay payload or a
  decrypted network token.
</Callout>

### Retrieve the stored payment method ID

How you retrieve the `pm_card_...` ID depends on the integration path used
for the initial Apple Pay payment.

<Tabs items={['SDK', 'API']}>
  <Tab value="SDK">
    Retrieve the `pm_card_...` ID from the `onSuccess` callback after the
    customer completes the initial Apple Pay payment:

```js
    const checkout = pay.checkout({
      clientSecret: '{client_secret}',
      onSuccess: (payment) => {
        const paymentMethodId = payment.payment_method
        // Store paymentMethodId against the customer in your database
      },
      onFailure: (error) => {
        console.error(error.message)
      },
    })
```
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="API">
    On the initial Apple Pay charge request, include
    `"setup_future_usage": "off_session"` inside your `source_data` object.
    This tells the issuing bank that the card will be used for future
    merchant-initiated transactions.

    The charge response returns the values you need for all future recurring
    charges:

```json
    {
      "id": "chrg_687257433738839040",
      "payment_method": "pm_card_687257443142470656",
      "underlying_network_id": "2858e1b8-c453-4ef9-8940-18c2e159725b",
      "status": "succeeded"
    }
```

    Store both the `payment_method` ID and the `underlying_network_id`
    against the customer record in your database. You will need these for
    future recurring charges.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

###  Create recurring charges or subscriptions

Once you have the `pm_card_...` ID, you can use it to charge the customer
without them being present. There are two paths depending on your billing
model:

- **Recurring charges** — trigger each charge manually using the stored
`pm_card_...` ID with `"off_session": true`. See the
[recurring charges guide](/docs/payments/server-to-server-guides/create-recurring-charges)
for the full request format, all targeting methods, response handling, and
webhook events.

- **Subscriptions** — attach the stored `pm_card_...` ID to a subscription
package and let Pay.com manage the billing schedule automatically. See the
[subscriptions guide](/docs/payments/sdks/create-recurring-charges-using-sessions)
for the full setup instructions.

<Callout type="warn">
 Apple Pay generates a new device-specific token on every
  transaction. Recurring charges are made against the underlying card that
  Pay.com extracts during the initial payment, not against the Apple Pay
  wallet itself.
</Callout>
