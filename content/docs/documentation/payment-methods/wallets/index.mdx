A digital wallet stores a tokenized version of the customer's payment credentials and authenticates                                                                                             
using a biometric or device passcode. Pay.com supports Apple Pay and Google Pay. When a customer selects                                                                                        
either wallet at checkout, an in-page payment sheet appears.                                                                                                                        

The wallet passes a cryptographic payment token to Pay.com instead of the underlying card number.                                                                                               
Your integration never handles raw card credentials. Wallets reduce checkout friction most on mobile,                                                                                           
where eliminating manual card entry reduces drop-off.   

## How it works

When a customer selects a wallet at checkout, the entire flow happens on your page. See how it works below:

1. The customer selects Apple Pay or Google Pay at checkout.
2. An in-page payment sheet opens. The customer does not leave your page.
3. The customer authenticates using a biometric (Face ID, Touch ID, or fingerprint) or their device passcode.
4. The wallet generates a cryptographic token (a network token with a transaction-specific cryptogram) and passes it to Pay.com. Your integration never receives the underlying card number.
5. Pay.com processes the payment. Confirmation is immediate and synchronous.

## When to offer digital wallets

Wallets work best for merchants with mobile-heavy checkout traffic. Apple Pay and Google Pay have wide global
adoption and provide a one-gesture checkout experience. Apple Pay is available on Apple devices (iPhone, iPad,
and Mac with Safari); Google Pay works on Android and Chrome on any platform. Customers need at least one card
saved to their wallet before checkout.

Wallet payments collapse checkout from several steps to a single biometric gesture. Customers pay without
typing a card number, expiry date, or CVV. The conversion benefit is most significant on mobile, where typing
accuracy is lower and form abandonment is higher.

Coverage varies by device and region. If your primary market has low wallet adoption you may add integration
complexity without a meaningful conversion gain. Support for recurring payments and merchant-initiated charges
is also limited. Before enabling wallets for subscriptions or saved-credential flows, confirm that the specific
wallet supports each of the following:

- Token creation for future use
- Merchant-initiated charges without re-authentication
- Automatic token updates when the underlying card is replaced

## Key properties

Both wallets share the following behavior and integration properties:

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Payment confirmation** | Immediate. Both wallets return a synchronous `succeeded` status. |
| **Settlement timing** | Next business day |
| **Refund support** | Full and partial |
| **Disputes** | Supported via the standard card chargeback process. Time window is card-network-dependent. |
| **Recurring payments** | Partial (see individual method pages) |
| **3DS / SCA** | Handled automatically. Apple Pay and Google Pay Cryptogram 3DS authenticate at the device level; no additional 3DS challenge is triggered. Google Pay `PAN_ONLY` may trigger a challenge, which Pay.com handles via the SDK. |
| **Redirect required** | No. Both wallets display an in-page sheet. |
| **Pay.com products supported** | Web SDK, REST API |

## Regulatory and geographic considerations

Wallet payment availability and compliance requirements vary by region and configuration. Review the following considerations before going live:

**Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)**

Apple Pay and Google Pay using the Cryptogram 3DS credential authenticate the customer at the device level via 
biometrics or device passcode. This satisfies the SCA requirement under PSD2 for transactions in the European 
Economic Area. No additional 3DS challenge is required. Pay.com passes the ECI indicator and wallet cryptogram 
directly to the issuer. For Google Pay `PAN_ONLY` transactions, which do not include device-level authentication, 
Pay.com's Agnostic 3DS layer applies automatically when using the SDK.

**Compliance obligations**

Apple Pay requires domain verification before the payment sheet will load in Safari. This step is independent of your Pay.com integration. Complete it before going live.

Google Pay requires you to set your gateway to `paycom` and your `gatewayMerchantId` to 
`mrchnt_{your_merchant_id}` in your Google Pay configuration object.

Both methods must be explicitly enabled in your Pay.com dashboard under **Settings → Payment Methods → 
Add Configuration Set** before they become available at checkout. This step requires Pay.com to activate the feature on your account.

## Product compatibility

The following table shows which Pay.com products support wallet payments and how each integration path works:

| | Web SDK | REST API | Payout | Recurring payments |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Apple Pay** | ✓ ¹ | ✓ ² | ✗ | ✗ |
| **Google Pay** | ✓ ¹ | ✓ ² | ✗ | ✗ |

¹ **Web SDK** supports two integration paths:
- **Universal component:** Renders all activated payment methods automatically. No additional configuration required 
beyond enabling the wallet in your dashboard.
- **Headless SDK:** Use `checkout.headless('applepay')` or `checkout.headless('googlepay')`. Requires Apple Pay 
merchant identifier or Google Pay merchant ID setup. See individual method pages.

² **REST API:** Create a charge with `source_data.type: "network_token"` and include the wallet token payload 
obtained from the Apple Pay or Google Pay session.
