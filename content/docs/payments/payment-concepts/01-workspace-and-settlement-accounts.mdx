title: Workspace and settlement accounts
description: Understand how transactions are organized through workspaces and how funds are routed to settlement accounts
---

The Pay.com architecture has a hierarchy of entities that allow you to separate operational management from the physical movement of funds. This structure provides the flexibility to manage multiple brands or regions under a single integration.

To understand how funds are organized, you must distinguish between the three primary layers of the platform:

* **Workspace:** Also referred to as a "Merchant Workspace," this is your central orchestration environment. It acts as a dashboard where you manage transaction logic, routing rules, and configurations.
* **Settlement account:** These represent the actual technical connections to acquirers. A settlement account is the specific endpoint where processed funds are eventually received.
* **Legal entity:** A background entity tied to your onboarding and compliance process. Every settlement account is linked to an entity for regulatory purposes.

## Workspace management

A workspace is a logical grouping that helps you organize data and permissions. The system is designed to be flexible, allowing your setup to be as simple or as complex as your business requires:

* You can use a single workspace to see all payments across all brands, entities, and providers in one dashboard.
* You can split workspaces based on geography, such as having a dedicated workspace for US customers and another for European markets.
* You can define workspaces based on what is most comfortable for your operations, whether that is separation by URL, brand, or country.

## Routing to settlement accounts

The power of the orchestration engine lies in how workspaces interact with settlement accounts. Because these entities are decoupled, you can integrate once and then dynamically route your traffic:

* A single workspace can share multiple settlement accounts, and a settlement account can be reused across different workspaces.
* You can add or change your acquirer connections through the dashboard without needing to update your frontend integration or API calls.
* Once a provider is configured, it creates a settlement account that can be used for routing rules immediately.
